Our Mission

This course was born out of a simple observation: artificial intelligence is already present in our classrooms, whether we invite it or not. Students are using it, employers are expecting familiarity with it, and the conversation around it is only growing louder. Rather than leave faculty to navigate that conversation alone, we set out to create a resource that meets educators where they are, is honest about AI's limitations, respectful of faculty concerns, and practical in its guidance.

Our goal is not to convince you that AI is good or bad, but to give you the knowledge and context to form your own informed perspective and make thoughtful decisions about how or whether it fits into your teaching.

A Note on How This Course Was Built
In the spirit of transparency that we ask of our students, we want to acknowledge that AI tools were used in the development of this course for drafting, researching, and organizing content. Every module was reviewed, shaped, and approved by our team. The ideas, structure, and pedagogical decisions reflect human judgment. We believe modeling responsible AI use is just as important as teaching it.
